JANUARY 1ST, 2030
In a world where climate change has become irreversible, the consequences are dire and far-reaching. Coastal cities face relentless inundation from rising sea levels, triggering mass migrations and exacerbating social tensions.
Warming waters in the oceans pose significant threats to marine ecosystems and human societies. They can lead to coral bleaching, disrupting vital habitats like coral reefs, and impacting fisheries as fish populations shift. Additionally, warming waters exacerbate ocean acidification, harming marine life with calcium carbonate shells or skeletons. The increased intensity of tropical storms fueled by warmer waters also poses risks to coastal communities and infrastructure. Furthermore, the loss of sea ice in polar regions affects unique ecosystems and indigenous communities. Addressing climate change is crucial to mitigate these impacts and safeguard the health and resilience of the oceans and the millions who depend on them.
